Common questions

Is copy trading profitable?
Copy trading can be profitable, but most retail accounts lose money when copy trading CFDs. The best outcomes come from diversifying across multiple traders, setting strict stop-losses, and copying traders with consistent 12+ month track records — not just short-term returns.
How much do I need to start?
eToro allows accounts from $50 (varies by country). ZuluTrade is broker-dependent — some partner brokers allow $0 minimums. In practice, $200–$500 gives you enough to copy multiple traders meaningfully.
Is copy trading legal?
Yes, copy trading is legal in most countries. All platforms reviewed here are regulated by major authorities (FCA, CySEC, ASIC). Regulation status varies by country — always check local rules.
Do I pay the trader I copy?
On eToro, no — the trader earns a bonus from eToro based on copiers, not from you directly. Some platforms do charge performance fees (typically 20% of profits). Always read the fee schedule before copying.
What's the difference between copy trading and mirror trading?
Copy trading replicates the live trades of a specific trader you choose. Mirror trading (or social trading signals) typically follows rule-based algorithmic strategies. Copy trading is more personal; mirror trading is more systematic.
